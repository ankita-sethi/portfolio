About

I’m a Data Engineer and Machine Learning Researcher passionate about building scalable data systems and applying AI to improve accessibility and real-world usability. My work combines data engineering, large language models (LLMs) and human-centered computing to develop reliable and socially impactful solutions.

As part of my graduate research at Stony Brook University, I developed AI-powered accessibility tools for visually impaired users by integrating LLMs with screen-reading systems. The project focused on creating intelligent assistants capable of context-aware responses, enhancing usability and autonomy for non-visual users.

Before graduate school, I worked as a Data Engineer at Accenture, where I designed and optimized ETL pipelines using Talend and Snowflake, automated workflows, and built a Python-SQL chatbot that connected backend data and improved scheduling.

I’m motivated by projects that merge AI innovation with human impact — from detecting hallucinations in LLMs to developing sustainable smart energy systems.

Beyond data and code, I’m usually perfecting an espresso, vlogging about food, or sketching UI ideas while relaxing with chill soundtracks.
