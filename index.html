About

I’m a Data Engineer and Machine Learning Researcher focused on building scalable data systems and applying AI to improve accessibility and real-world usability. My work blends data engineering, large language models (LLMs) and human-centered computing to create reliable and socially meaningful solutions.

During my graduate research at Stony Brook University, I developed AI-powered accessibility tools for visually impaired users by integrating LLMs with screen-reading systems. I built context-aware assistants that improved navigation, comprehension and autonomy for non-visual users.

Before graduate school, I worked as a Data Engineer at Accenture, where I designed and optimized ETL pipelines using Talend and Snowflake, automated workflows and built a Python and SQL based chatbot that improved scheduling and support efficiency across teams.

I’m motivated by projects that combine AI innovation with human impact from detecting hallucinations in LLMs to developing sustainable smart energy systems.

Outside of work, I enjoy perfecting espresso, creating food videos and sketching UI ideas while relaxing with music.
